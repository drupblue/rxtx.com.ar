Sitio de cliches argumentales

22 Sep 2008

Este topic no tiene mucho de tecnico, pero bastante de geeky.

TVTropes.org es un wiki que lista clichés argumentales de todo tipo, incluyeno películas, libros, videojuegos, etc etc. Cada cliché o "trope" incluye una descripción y una lista de ejemplos del mismo, donde es aplicado, modificado o incluso utilizado de forma opuesta.

Extracto algunos ejemplos:

Taking The Bullet
A form of Heroic Sacrifice where, when the Big Bad fires a weapon at someone and another character leaps into the path of the bullet, receiving the injury instead. Time will often slow to a crawl, and the leaper will often shout a Big No. The leaper will then have a scene where they weeping-ly tell the character how important they are before losing consciousness, while cradled in the arms of the survivor. Sometimes they get better, sometimes not, but it's close, either way. Usually results in tears and/or an angry How Dare You Die On Me moment, especially if the person they took the bullet for loved them in any way....

Redemption Equals Death
If a character has done something horrible, or unforgivable, and in the course of fighting to undo the damage is redeemed, or rights their situation, they will die. Badly.

Obi Wan Moment
Not everyone in fiction goes out the same way. While some people may scream, curse and rage or act terrified, for some characters that sort of thing is just... out of character. So instead their death is an unusually calm moment, where they take their last few seconds and use them to reassure the people they're leaving behind or find one last moment of peace before they Go Into The Light. Sometimes they just smile at their surviving companion.

Disney Death
Beloved major character is seemingly killed at the climax of the movie/episode, hearts are wrenched, four-year-olds are traumatized, and then — oh look, they're Not Quite Dead after all.

The Hooker With The Heart Of Gold
This is a sex worker (Prostitute, Stripper, Porno Star) who becomes a love interest for a main character who really should know better. Turns out, underneath the sex kitten exterior there's a sweet, innocent flower that needs nurturing.The Pretty Woman is almost always killed off by an angry pimp, as it seems to make producers uncomfortable to have their characters form a lasting relationship with the character. This usually ends up feeling like An Aesop.

Private Eye Monologue
The signature narration style in Film Noir. A bored lookin' tough guy with his feet on the desk meets a Femme Fatale, while the voiceover gives us the mental play-by-play. The Private Eye Monologue is characterized by certain pronunciation and speech patterns that make it immediately recognizable and utterly cool. The most basic rule to remember is that it is a monologue, so it is spoken (not written), preferably in a deep chain-smoker baritone. An emphasis is always placed on the last (or second last) word in the sentence, to make clear where it ends.

The Butler Did It
A Dead Horse Trope in murder mysteries, which was never alive to begin with. Or, to be more precise, a Dead Unicorn Trope: subversions, which are said to be plentiful by people prone to understatement, tend to assume that this trope was once often used straight, although it never was.
The stereotypical example would be that a bunch of people are invited to a dinner in a wealthy man's house, and the wealthy man is poisoned while they are all eating dinner. All the guests debate who among them is the killer, only to discover at the end of the story that the killer is the butler, who nobody bothered to think twice about.
